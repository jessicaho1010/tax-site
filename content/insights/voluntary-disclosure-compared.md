Not all voluntary disclosure programs are created equal. For taxpayers with unreported assets or income spanning multiple countries, understanding the differences between national programs is essential to designing a disclosure strategy that works.

## United States

The IRS operates two primary voluntary compliance pathways:

### Streamlined Filing Compliance Procedures (SFCP)
- **Eligibility:** U.S. individual taxpayers (including dual citizens) whose failure to report was non-willful
- **Disclosure period:** 3 years of tax returns, 6 years of FBARs
- **Penalties:** Waiver of late-filing and FBAR penalties; only tax and interest due
- **Key advantage:** No penalty exposure — but eligibility requires a credible non-willfulness certification

### Voluntary Disclosure Practice (VDP)
- **Eligibility:** Taxpayers with willful non-compliance or criminal exposure
- **Disclosure period:** 6 years (generally)
- **Penalties:** Civil fraud penalty (75% of the highest year's tax deficiency) or FBAR miscellaneous penalty; no guaranteed outcome
- **Key advantage:** Provides a pathway to resolve criminal exposure, but the cost is significant

## United Kingdom

HMRC's **Worldwide Disclosure Facility (WDF)** and **Contractual Disclosure Facility (CDF)** offer tiered approaches:

- **WDF:** For non-deliberate non-compliance. Requires a full disclosure with penalty mitigation. Penalties range from 0% to 100% of the tax depending on behavior (careless vs. deliberate).
- **CDF (Code of Practice 9):** For cases involving suspected fraud. Acceptance into CDF means HMRC will generally not pursue criminal investigation, though this is not guaranteed.

## Key EU Jurisdictions

### Germany
Voluntary disclosure (*Selbstanzeige*) under § 371 AO is available but has been significantly tightened. Full disclosure of all unreported income for the last 10 years is required, and a 5% surcharge applies above certain thresholds. Critically, the disclosure must be complete across all tax years — partial disclosure provides no protection.

### France
The *Service de Traitement des Déclarations Rectificatives* (STDR) was closed in 2017. Currently, France offers no formal voluntary disclosure program — taxpayers must engage directly with the tax authority on a case-by-case basis.

### Netherlands
The Dutch *inkeerregeling* (voluntary improvement scheme) allows taxpayers to correct prior returns. For income tax, the lookback period is generally 5 years, with a penalty of up to 60% of the corrected tax (reduced if voluntary). For foreign assets, the penalty regime under Box 3 requires careful navigation.

## Practical Considerations for Multi-Jurisdiction Cases

1. **First-mover advantage matters less than coherence.** If you disclose in one jurisdiction, authorities in other jurisdictions may learn of it. Your strategy must account for this.
2. **Treaty protections vary.** Mutual assistance provisions in double tax treaties can facilitate information exchange between countries — voluntary disclosure in Country A does not guarantee Country B won't find out independently.
3. **Residency is critical.** Your tax residency status in each relevant jurisdiction determines which disclosure programs are available to you. Dual residents face the most complex analysis.

## Conclusion

Voluntary disclosure remains one of the most effective tools for resolving historical tax non-compliance. But the complexity of coordinating disclosures across multiple jurisdictions cannot be overstated. Professional guidance tailored to your specific facts is essential.
